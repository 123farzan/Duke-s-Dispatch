Dukes Dispatch started in a one-room office in 2006 with three trucks and a CB radio. Twenty years later, we're still owner-operator owned and operated — and we still answer the phone ourselves.

— THE STORY

We got tired of being burned by dispatchers too.

Before we ran Dukes, we ran trucks. We paid 10% to dispatchers who never picked up the phone, who loaded us on deadhead-heavy lanes, who ghosted when detention came up, and who locked us into year-long contracts we couldn't get out of.

So in 2006, we built the dispatch service we wished we'd had as owner-operators. One personal dispatcher per carrier. Flat fees, no percentages. No contracts, no forced dispatch. A real human on the other end of the radio at 2am.

Twenty years later, that's still the entire business model. We've never raised rates beyond cost-of-living. We've never missed payroll to a partner. We've never quit on a carrier who needed us at 3am on a Sunday.

We're not the biggest dispatch service. We're not trying to be. We're the one you call when you want your dispatcher to actually know your name.

— OUR PROMISE

What we will never do.

It helps to be clear about the things we won't do, because most of our competitors do them.

  • We will never take a percentage of your load pay.
  • We will never force a load on you that you don't want.
  • We will never lock you into a long-term contract.
  • We will never charge a setup fee, an admin fee, or a "fuel card fee."
  • We will never send you to a broker we haven't credit-checked.
  • We will never hide a detention claim we could have fought for.
  • We will never route your call through a phone tree.
