— THE STRAIGHT ANSWERS

What operators ask first.

Do you take a percentage of my load pay?

Only if you want us to. We offer two pricing tracks: a fixed weekly fee OR a percentage of your weekly revenue. You pick the one that fits your operation — and you can switch any week. The fixed-fee plan is what most carriers pick, and on that plan you keep more control of your weekly result. The revenue-share plan is opt-in and tied to the revenue you generate.

Can I really turn down loads?

Yes. That's the whole point of "no forced dispatch." If the rate is bad, the lane is bad, or you just don't want it — say so. We'll find you one you do want.

Am I locked into a contract?

No. We work week-to-week. Quit on a Tuesday, you're not paying for next Tuesday. We earn your business every week — and we know it.

What if I'm new — no MC, no authority yet?

We help with that too. MC authority setup, EIN, IRP, IFTA, UCR — the paperwork maze that takes most new carriers 6 weeks, we can cut to 2. Add it to the Professional plan.

What's your avg rate per mile?

Depends on equipment and lane, but our carriers averaged $2.85/mi last quarter — that's nearly 50¢ better than the load-board average. The real number for your rig comes from a 5-minute call.

Where are your dispatchers located?

Harrisonville, Missouri — and a few in Texas. All US-based. No overseas call centers. When you call, you talk to your dispatcher, not a queue.
